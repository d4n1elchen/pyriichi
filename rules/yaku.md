# Yaku Rules

## Basic Yaku

- Riichi: closed hand, tenpai after declaration discard, one Riichi Stick paid.
- Ippatsu: win within one uninterrupted turn after riichi.
- Menzen Tsumo: tsumo with a fully concealed hand.
- Tanyao: all tiles are simples, 2-8. Open Tanyao is ruleset-dependent.
- Pinfu: sequence hand with a non-value pair and no fu from sets; ryanmen requirement is ruleset-dependent.
- Iipeikou: fully concealed hand with two identical sequences.
- Yakuhai: triplet or kan of round_wind, seat_wind, haku, hatsu, or chun.
- Haitei: win by tsumo on the last live wall tile.
- Houtei: win by ron on the last discard.
- Rinshan: win by tsumo on a rinshan tile after kan.
- Chankan: win by robbing another player's kan.

## Special Yaku

- Double Riichi: declare riichi on the first uninterrupted turn, 2 han.
- Sanshoku Doujun: same-number sequence in all three suits, 2 han closed and 1 han open.
- Sanshoku Doukou: same-number triplet in all three suits, 2 han.
- Ittsu: 1-3, 4-6, and 7-9 sequences in one suit, 2 han closed and 1 han open.
- Toitoi: all sets are triplets or kans, 2 han.
- Sanankou: three concealed triplets, 2 han.
- Sankantsu: three kans, 2 han.
- Shousangen: two dragon triplets or kans and one dragon pair, 2 han.
- Honroutou: all tiles are terminals and honors, 2 han.
- Chiitoitsu: seven pairs, 2 han.
- Renhou: non-dealer first-turn ron. Value is ruleset-dependent.

## High-Value Yaku

- Chinitsu: all tiles from one suit, 6 han closed and 5 han open.
- Honitsu: one suit plus honors, 3 han closed and 2 han open.
- Junchan: every set contains a terminal and there are no honors, 3 han closed and 2 han open.
- Chanta: every set contains a terminal or honor, 2 han closed and 1 han open.
- Ryanpeikou: two Iipeikou patterns, 3 han.

## Yakuman

- Tenhou: dealer wins from the initial hand.
- Chihou: non-dealer wins by first-turn tsumo.
- Daisangen: triplets or kans of all three dragons.
- Shousuushi: three wind triplets or kans plus a wind pair.
- Daisuushi: four wind triplets or kans.
- Suuankou: four concealed triplets.
- Suuankou Tanki: Suuankou with a single wait; double yakuman is ruleset-dependent.
- Suukantsu: four kans.
- Chuuren Poutou: special Chinitsu pattern.
- Pure Chuuren Poutou: true nine-sided Chuuren Poutou pattern; double yakuman is ruleset-dependent.
- Kokushi Musou: one of each terminal and honor plus one duplicate.
- Kokushi Musou Juusanmen: thirteen-sided Kokushi Musou wait; double yakuman is ruleset-dependent.
- Ryuuiisou: all green tiles.
- Chinroutou: all terminals.
- Tsuuiisou: all honors.

Optional local yakuman and legacy variants may be configured separately, but they are not part of the standard modern yaku list.

## Combination Rules

- Chiitoitsu cannot combine with standard-shape yaku such as Pinfu, Iipeikou, or Toitoi.
- Chiitoitsu can combine with compatible composition yaku such as Tanyao, Honitsu, Chinitsu, Honroutou, Riichi, Menzen Tsumo, and dora.
- Pinfu cannot combine with Yakuhai pair value or Toitoi.
- Pinfu can combine with Iipeikou or Ryanpeikou when the hand also satisfies Pinfu.
- Tanyao cannot combine with yaku that require terminals or honors.
- Toitoi cannot combine with sequence yaku.
- Iipeikou and Ryanpeikou are mutually exclusive.
- Chinitsu and Honitsu are mutually exclusive.
- Junchan and Chanta are mutually exclusive.
- Yakuman do not score together with non-yakuman yaku.
- Multiple yakuman may combine.
